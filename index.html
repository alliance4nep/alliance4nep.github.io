What is Covid Alliance For Nepal?

Covid Alliance For Nepal is a group of individuals and organizations who have come together to help the country respond to the second wave of COVID-19 from local to national level. This group is a collaborative initiative open to any individual or organization willing to contribute to this relief effort. To efficiently manage the efforts, we have created groups within the Alliance to tackle different aspects of the relief work. Currently, we are working to tackle the Covid crisis especially in the areas of minimizing spread, reducing the number of patients being hospitalized, supporting treatment, and providing crisis economic support.

What are we doing?

We are currently working on three major projects where we saw the most imminent need and gaps.

Telemedicine Hotline

A hotline responding to calls looking for medical support or referrals; this includes a telemedicine referral. In our first day of operation, we received 50 calls -- and while we are glad we were able to respond, it is painful to know the reasons why;

Oxygen for Nepal

Oxygen cylinder tracking and circulation/transport support: A major issues right now is that people (and industries) are hoarding cylinders. While there is enough oxygen, it can't be delivered to those who need supplemental oxygen, because of this. We are setting up efficient tracking and distribution channels, where transport and oxygen refill and circulation times are managed to maximize the use of each oxygen cylinder. With Creasion Nepal, we are coordinating efforts to procure 500 well-maintained cylinders within the month of May, from place including neighboring countries.

Isolation Center

Strategic support for isolation centers: we recently created a "Blueprint," based on the Nepal government's outline, in collaboration with medical doctors who have been treating COVID patients in contexts around the world. We've shared and will continue to share this with individuals and groups setting up surge management/isolation centers in Nepal. Weâ€™ve been working with local government representatives to understand what they require to set up and run isolation centers and create a platform for the private sector to step up and help.

Our team has also been working to identify potential isolation centers and set it up with required resources with food, telemedicine support, staff training, and oxygen supplies for those that need it. Currently, we are supporting an isolation center with food and oxygen cylinders at Tokha that has been set up in collaboration with the local government.

Working with the government

We've been working with local government representatives to understand what they require to set up and run isolation centers and create a platform for private sectors to help with what is required for that matter.

Welfare Fund

Economic impact assistance: we're establishing a means by which daily wage laborers, members of marginalized communities, and people who may be having other medical emergencies, can access a fund. We are still doing outreach for this. We are also working with key banking professionals to set up a welfare fund for frontline workers at isolation centers for if they get sick and need monetary and medical support for Covid-related medical expenses, for up to 4 months, for up to 1 lakh per person.

Alongside this we are actively campaigning on two following issues:

Vaccines for Nepal

Vaccine advocacy and lobbying by pushing actors with the power to (re)allocate their surplus vaccine dose, and to work towards making sure vulnerable communities in Nepal receive this intervention. For more information see www.vaccinesfornepal.org, which includes our recent letter to the US Ambassador to Nepal ;

Mental Wellbeing:

Given the current situation is causing a lot of stress, anxiety, and other issues related to the mind, we are organizing various online sessions to help people manage their emotional and mental wellbeing. We are working with mental health organizations to create support group networks and access to relevant and qualified resources.
