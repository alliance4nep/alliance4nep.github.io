What is Covid Alliance For Nepal?

Covid Alliance For Nepal is a group of individuals and organizations who have come together to help the country respond to the second wave of COVID-19 from local to national level. This group is a collaborative initiative open to any individual or organization willing to contribute in this relief effort. To efficiently manage the efforts, we have created groups within the Alliance to tackle different aspects of the relief work. Currently we are working to tackle the Covid crisis especially in the areas of minimizing spread, reducing the number of patients being hospitalized, supporting treatment, and providing crisis economic support.

Why are we doing this?

Our goal is to minimize tremendous suffering and loss of life over the coming weeks. We want to complement efforts by the government and Nepali hospitals to reduce the burden of hospital visits and other harms. Our areas of action are:

Telemedicine Hotline (988-003-5000)

A hotline responding to patient and caregivers calls looking for medical support or referrals staffed by trained nurses and medical officers has been up and running for a few days already. On a daily basis we receive more than 100 calls. Please refer to this dashboard for daily stats. Given the volume, we have trained and recruited additional medical members, and added hours of operation to cover the call distribution times. While we are glad we were able to respond, it is painful to know the reasons why. As the crisis deepens we also receive a lot requests for ICU beds which we put up requests on www.covidconnectnp.org, and for oxygen supply, to another Alliance initiative OxygenforNepal and other initiatives. Furthermore, we keep track of calls that we believe need economic assistance and follow up with the caller and if deemed necessary connect them to the Alliance members working on food relief or guide them to the nearest distribution center that we know of. ASK Foundation and Danphe Care are leading this effort. To support this initiative please go to ASK Foundation.

Oxygen for Nepal

Oxygen cylinder tracking and circulation/transport support: A major issue right now is that people (and industries) are hoarding cylinders. We are setting up efficient tracking and distribution channels, where transport and oxygen refill and circulation times are managed to maximize the use of each oxygen cylinder. With Creasion Nepal, we are coordinating efforts to procure 5000 well-maintained cylinders, as well as concentrators and oxygen plants within the month of May, from places including neighboring countries. For more information see the official page

Isolation Center

Strategic support for isolation centers: we recently created a "Blueprint," based on the Nepal government's outline, in collaboration with medical doctors who have been treating COVID patients in contexts around the world. We've shared and will continue to share this with individuals and groups setting up surge management/isolation centers in Nepal. We’ve been working with local government representatives to understand what they require to set up and run isolation centers and create a platform for the private sector to step up and help. If you are advocating for such a center, and want these materials, please just ask!

Our team has also been working to identify potential isolation centers and set it up with required resources with food, telemedicine support, staff training, and oxygen supplies for those that need it. Currently, we are supporting an isolation center with food and oxygen cylinders at Tokha that has been set up in collaboration with the local government.

Working with the government

We've been working with local government representatives to understand what they require to set up and run isolation centers and create a platform for the private sector to help with what is required for that matter.

Welfare Fund

Economic impact assistance: we're establishing a means by which daily wage laborers, members of marginalized communities, and people who may be having other medical emergencies, can access a fund. We are still doing outreach for this. We are also working with key banking professionals to set up a welfare fund for frontline workers at isolation centers for if they get sick and need monetary and medical support for Covid-related medical expenses, for up to 4 months, for up to 1 lakh per person.

Vaccines for Nepal

Vaccine advocacy and lobbying by pushing actors with the power to (re)allocate their surplus vaccine dose, and to work towards making sure vulnerable communities in Nepal receive this intervention. For more information see www.vaccinesfornepal.org, which includes our recent letter to the US Ambassador to Nepal ;

Mental Wellbeing

Given the current situation is causing a lot of stress, anxiety, and other issues related to the mind, we are organizing various online sessions to help people manage their emotional and mental wellbeing. We are working with mental health organizations to create support group networks and access to relevant and qualified resources.

What is happening?

Nepal currently has the highest rate of reproduction of COVID-19 in the world. In some places, test results - for those who take them - are returning with positivity rates of up to 40%. By midJuly, at the current rate of reproduction and infection, Nepal could be looking at 800,000 Covid patients.

Our public health and medical infrastructure is strained and insufficient even in ordinary times, but the dearth of trained doctors, hospital access, ICUs, and even basic materials like supplementary oxygen is one that we feel especially acutely now. The “low” mortality rates for Covid are in contexts with medical infrastructure and supplementary oxygen that can respond to patient need, and avert death. We are already looking at a possibility of 10 critically ill patients for every hospital bed, and where supplies of otherwise-basic medication, and oxygen, are already running very low, and not accessible in other places. From the strict lockdown of 2020, Nepalis have been facing lockdown fatigue -- but at entirely too dangerous a time.

Breaking the cycle of nation-wide lockdowns can only take place with vaccination and mass public health advocacy. While the “situation” in terms of infection and transmission is sobering, we are committed to working with each other, and other groups, to mobilize what we can to respond to peoples’ needs.
