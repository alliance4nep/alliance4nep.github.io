What is Covid Alliance For Nepal?

Covid Alliance For Nepal is a group of individuals and organizations who have come together to help the country respond to the second wave of COVID-19 from local to national level. This group is a collaborative initiative open to any individual or organization willing to contribute to this relief effort. To efficiently manage the efforts, we have created groups within the Alliance to tackle different aspects of the relief work. Currently, we are working to tackle the Covid crisis especially in the areas of minimizing spread, reducing the number of patients being hospitalized, supporting treatment, and providing crisis economic support.

What are we doing?

We are currently working on three major projects where we saw the most imminent need and gaps.

Telemedicine Hotline

We have trained medical teams that have been supporting the Nepali people by providing general information regarding COVID-19 as well as appropriate telemedicine guidance and directing them to the proper resources to help manage/treat COVID 19. The medical team will provide consultation to manage COVID-19 positive cases at home when possible, and identify the urgency for hospitalization to reduce the load of unnecessary hospitalization.

Oxygen for Nepal

  • Given the scarcity of oxygen gas cylinders in Nepal, we are coordinating efforts to procure 500 oxygen cylinders within the month of May.
  • We are setting up efficient tracking and distribution channels, where transport and oxygen refill and circulation times are managed to maximize the use of each oxygen cylinder.
  • We are seeking well-maintained oxygen cylinder donations from neighboring countries to reduce the cost of procurement.

Isolation Center

Our team has also been working to identify potential isolation centers and set them up with the required resources for it. Currently, we are supporting an isolation center with food and oxygen cylinders at Tokha that has been set up in collaboration with the local government. Our work regarding isolation centers is divided into the following aspects.

Working with the government

We've been working with local government representatives to understand what they require to set up and run isolation centers and create a platform for private sectors to help with what is required for that matter.

Welfare Fund

We are working with key banking professionals to create a welfare fund that will cover any Covid related medical expenses for the frontline workers of the isolation center for the duration of 4 months (while they are working) of up to 1 lakh per person.

Mental Wellbeing:

Given the current situation is causing a lot of stress, anxiety, and other issues related to the mind, we are organizing various online sessions to help people manage their emotional and mental wellbeing. We are working with mental health organizations to create support group networks and access to relevant and qualified resources.
